Motherhood

A networking app for mothers of young children.

Overview

The Motherhood app was a concept developed for the course “User Experience I: Survey at UCLA Extension. The assignment was to create a Login and Create Account feature for a networking site to help moms with young kids.

  • Client: Class project
  • Role: UX/UI Designer & Researcher
  • Tools: Adobe XD
  • Duration: 3 months

Problem

Originally the assignment was for a social networking app for mothers. Which brings to mind sites like Facebook, LinkedIn or even dating sites like Bumble, but after interviewing 7 mothers I found that:

  • They wanted the choice to be anonymous.
  • Meeting new people seemed scary/risky.
  • They're too busy to meet new people.
  • They'd like to talk to parents who can relate.

So while, all of the mothers expressed wanting to talk with parents who’ve had similar experiences in order to make sure their child’s development was healthy and normal, they really weren’t looking to network in the traditional sense.

Solution

Since the users were more interested in getting information and casually talking to people, I referenced online groups, forums and sites like Scary Mommy, Reddit and even Peloton groups.

The app I created would have articles about parenting and a forum where they can post questions or vent. They would also be able to post under whatever username and have control over their privacy.

Research

The main audience for this app are people who are environmentally conscious and ethical consumers. From our interviews, we found that there were four types of users, ranging from the complete newbie to the more experienced activist.

The main user we focused on was Greta who is somewhere in the middle. Like most of the people interviewed, she is already taking steps to reduce her carbon footprint, but wants to do more by making responsible purchases.
